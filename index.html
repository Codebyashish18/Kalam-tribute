Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India.

Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist who played a pivotal role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs. Born into a humble family in Rameswaram, Tamilnadu his journey from selling newspapers as a child to becoming the 11th President of India is a true inspiration.

Presidency and Leadership

During his tenure as the President of India (2002-2007), Dr. Kalam was widely admired as the "people's President" for his humility, accessibility, and focus on national development through technology and education. He broke the barriers between politicians and citizens, especially the youth.

Passion for Students

Dr. Kalam believed that the youth are the most powerful resource of any nation. He often said, "Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action." He visited thousands of schools and colleges to inspire students to innovate and lead with intergrity.

Key Achievements:

  • President of India(2002-2007).
  • Played key role in India's missile development program.
  • Awarded Bharat Ratna in 1997.
  • Authored best-selling motivational books.
  • Known as the "People's President".
